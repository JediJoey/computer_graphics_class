## Computer Graphics Class
### How do I approach designing software?
I prefer the DMAIC approach for anything I am engineering, including software.  Define, Measure, Analyze, Implement, Control.
Define - Define the customer requirements for the project.
Measure - Figure out options for how to solve the problem.
Analyze - Compare potential solutions and select the one that fits constraints best.
Implement - Do the work.
Control - Verify the problem is solved, won't happen again, and stakeholders are informed/satisfied (some people will never be happy).

### How do I approach developing programs?
I like to ask stakeholders a lot of questions until I am confident I understand their needs.  Depending on the stakeholder and scope I will draft either an SOW or document to show the scope that can be agreed on and referenced later.  Then I create an MVP/PoC very quickly to show them, then iterate until completed.  If iteration smells like scope creep then I will narrow the focus, reference the scope doc if needed, and pin them down to have a finish line.  Every stakeholder is different though, success here is usually defined by communication and relationships.
